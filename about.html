Practice

Good architecture is measured by the degree to which a design meets the sponsor’s expressed functions, economies, and aspirations, and the extent to which its inhabitants accept, understand, and delight in its physicality and being. It is rooted in the designer’s being deeply conscious of its purpose, available resources, context, history, precedents, and the designer’s ability to synthesize these myriad variables into a coherent, enlightened, and functioning whole.

Great architecture does the same, but is measured by the degree to which it is responsible, produced with integrity, goes beyond the expected, points the way forward, teaches us something new, and – ultimately – whether its lessons preserve value and remain relevant over time, context, and generations of the people who inhabit it. This temporal dimension to great architecture is in turn rooted in the visionary abilities of architects and their collaborators.

We’re committed to the path of great architecture, regardless of the scale and scope of any project.

This commitment arises from our belief in the singularity of a project’s requirements, its context, and its aspirations. The firm seeks to promote a broad awareness of the natural and built environment, the value of professional integrity, and the importance of a process that creates great personal satisfaction and fulfillment for its client, the public, and the firm.

Firm

Leandro V. Locsin Partners is the current incarnation of an unbroken and continuing architectural practice founded in 1955 by Leandro V. Locsin, Philippine National Artist for Architecture.

One of its earliest commissions, a high rise apartment building on Ayala Avenue, set the architectural idiom for subsequent buildings on what was then the frontier of urban development. The firm is credited with having helped shape Manila’s skyline and architectural landscapes.

Today, the firm’s influence extends beyond Ayala Avenue. From its drawing boards have sprung notable work such as the Chapel of the Holy Sacrifice at the University of the Philippines, the Cultural Center of the Philippines Complex, the 1970 Philippine Pavilion in Osaka, the Philippine International Convention Center, the Manila Hotel Redevelopment, the old Mandarin Oriental Hotel, the Manila International Airport, The first Citibank Building, the Makati and Philippine Stock Exchanges, the Anvaya Cove Resort Community, and the Ayala Museum (old and new).

Since 1955, the firm has designed over 33 public buildings, 75 commercial buildings, 6 hotels, 13 churches, several country clubs and museums, and more than 100 residences. The firm has also worked on the New Istana Nurul Iman State Palace and Seat of Government for the Sultan of Brunei in Bandar Seri Begawan. Encompassing a total floor area of approximately 200,000 square meters, the palace has the distinction of being the world's largest presidential residence.

With a staff that includes 40 dedicated architects and designers, the firm continues to immerse itself in the design traditions of the Philippines and Asia along with key developments in design theory, sustainability, and contemporary technology. The reconciliation of vernacular contexts and Western processes is strongly reflected in the firm’s work.
